What to test first

Start where live tests are most expensive: early learning, hook discovery, and concept direction — then use spend for verification and scaling.

Hook strength

See whether attention is captured in the opening seconds, and which visual idea actually earns the first look.

Pacing and structure

Identify moments that sustain engagement versus moments that leak attention, then refine timing, payoffs, and sequencing.

Drop-off diagnosis

Pinpoint the second where viewers disengage and tie it to the exact frame, scene change, or message shift.

Why this matters now

Live testing alone

  • High CPM/CPI makes significant tests expensive
  • Early metrics can be noisy, leading to false negatives
  • Privacy limits reduce visibility into down-funnel impact
  • Explains outcomes, not why the asset worked

Emhance AI

  • Experience insight: what was seen, when interest rose, where it dropped
  • Find growth gems inside campaigns, by identifying engaging moments
  • Informed direction: improve iteration before spending for certainty
  • Shared evidence: align UA, creative, and ASO on what to explore next

Method framing (disciplined)

Attention: gaze and blink dynamics indicate what is actually seen. Activation: facial expression intensity used as a proxy for emotional intensity. Timing: signals mapped second-by-second. Interpretation: used diagnostically, not as a verdict. Signals inform direction, not creative value judgements.
